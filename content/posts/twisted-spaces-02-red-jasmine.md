I knew not the value of flowers
till I chanced upon the scent of familiar jasmine.
I stumbled into the shop.
A small man burrowed into his computer.
I commented, as best I could, on the jasmine —
and the man, without looking up, excitedly declaimed:
Proud jasmines, from the Middle East, sir.
I could but laugh as he looked up, and recoiled.
I told him there were once jasmines in my country too.
But now they've all turned red.

— Shayer
