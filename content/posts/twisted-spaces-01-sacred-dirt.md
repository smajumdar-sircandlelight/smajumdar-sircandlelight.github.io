They opened my bags,<br>
Torn, battered, they held nothing,<br>
But dried fear, and a few torn shirts<br>
<br>
As they waved their metal sticks,<br>
To discern whether I was 'safe',<br>
They found my mother's old powder box<br>
<br>
Beautiful, ornate, and distinctly Persian in style,<br>
Yet, it contained nothing but dirt,<br>
And so, the gatekeeper looked at me curiously,<br>
<br>
I said not a word, for alas!<br>
How could he know?<br>
The legacy of the dirt was sacred to me,<br>
<br>
For that little, now, was home.<br>
<br>
<em>— Shayer</em>
